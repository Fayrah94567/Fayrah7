Activities & Background

I am currently a student at Dalian American International School (DAIS), where I am pursuing a double major in Psychology and Media with a concentration in Digital New Media.

One of the projects I am most proud of is an independent research project on appearance anxiety among teenagers. Inspired by my own observations, I explored how social media can contribute to appearance-related concerns. Through surveys and interviews, I examined the negative effects of online content and connected my findings to concepts such as algorithm-driven recommendations and filter bubbles, showing how personalized content can reinforce unrealistic beauty standards. This project allowed me to combine my interests in psychology and digital media while addressing a real-world social issue.

Outside the classroom, I co-founded a Creative Exploration Club with one of my classmates. Together, we organize free weekly art activities for children in our local community. We design a new theme each week to encourage creativity, curiosity, and hands-on learning, giving every child the opportunity to explore different forms of artistic expression.

In 2025, I also participated in a business competition focused on entrepreneurship, where my team worked on developing innovative business ideas and presenting practical solutions to real-world challenges. The experience strengthened my teamwork, communication, and problem-solving skills.

In addition, I volunteered as a museum docent, introducing visitors to the biology and habitat of brown bears. This experience helped me become more confident in public speaking and taught me how to communicate scientific knowledge in an engaging and accessible way.

Through these academic projects, community activities, competitions, and volunteer experiences, I have developed a deeper appreciation for understanding people, communicating ideas effectively, and making a positive impact in my community.
